Chandler Christian Community Center

Established 1966

Mission

At Chandler Christian Community Center, we change lives by nourishing minds and bodies to create a connected, thriving community.

Vision

To have a healthy and productive community built on a foundation of dignity and hope.

Our History

The Chandler Christian Community Center was organized in 1966 for the purpose of providing assistance to those who were in need. The center has provided emergency food to the needy of this community. The numbers have increased dramatically over the years. Today it remains the largest charitable Food Bank organization in the community, providing food to the low income and homeless in the area.
  • Nov. 6, 1966: CCCC was dedicated by Governor Sam Goddard, Mayor George Nader and other community members.
  • Jan. of 1968: CCCC was incorporated as a 501(c) 3nonprofit
  • 1969: volunteers picked up groceries from the local Basha’s grocery store along with beans and flour from the Tempe Flour Mill.
  • 1976: the California Street Baptist Church donated its property at 345 S. California Street and the CCCC moved to this location. Mrs. Al Barnes, owner of the Chandler Arizonan, local newspaper, at that time, provided funds for the purchase of the building.
  • 1990: United Way became a partner
  • 1991: 25th Anniversary Celebration
  • 1996: CCCC obtained its first walk in refrigerator/freezer.
  • 1996: 17,000 people provided food boxes. In 2012, 50,000 provided food boxes.
  • 2006: 40th Anniversary Celebration
